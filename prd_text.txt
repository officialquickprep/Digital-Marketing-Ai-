AI Brain — How It Works (RAG + LLM)
The AI brain is not just one model. It is a combination of a Large Language Model (LLM), a
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system, and a decision engine that routes tasks to the right agent.
What is the LLM?
 A Large Language Model (like GPT-4 or Claude) is the core reasoning engine
 It reads business context, understands goals, and makes strategic decisions
 It generates content, writes ad copy, crafts support replies, and interprets analytics
 It acts as the 'brain' that tells all 7 agents what to do
What is RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)?
 RAG is a technique where the AI retrieves specific business context before generating output
 Instead of using only general knowledge, the AI fetches the specific business's: menu, tone of voice,
past campaigns, customer data, brand guidelines
 This is stored in a Vector Database (Pinecone or Weaviate) as embeddings
 Result: AI generates content that is specific to THIS business, not generic
Example: RAG in Action for a Restaurant
Without RAG: AI writes 'Great pizza! Come visit us.'
With RAG: AI writes 'Our wood-fired Margherita Pizza is freshly made every day at our Jubilee Hills
location. Book a table tonight!'
RAG pulls: restaurant name, location, signature dish, brand tone → output is specific and authentic.
